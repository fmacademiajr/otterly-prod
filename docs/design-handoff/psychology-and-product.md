## 1. What Otterly is

Otterly is a calm task-starter for people with ADHD and chronic overwhelm. Philippines-first, iOS launch.

- Positioning line: **"Otterly: Start, Not Alone."** Subtitle: "Calm ADHD task starter."
- The wedge, and the whole thesis: **task lists are the disease, not the cure.** Every other productivity app hands you a longer list. Otterly's home screen shows ONE thing, never a list, never a red badge.
- The category is not "productivity app." It is "the thing you open when you cannot start." The competitor is not Todoist. The competitor is the frozen moment and the shame that follows it.
- Tone: warm, low-stimulation, no shame, no hustle, no guilt. It never punishes a bad week.

## 2. Who it is for

The involuntary-collapse audience — people with high motivation who cannot cross the intention-action gap. NOT people who are "lazy" (the framework is explicit that laziness is a different, voluntary, low-distress state; Otterly's user is in distress and wants to act).

Primary segments:
- The ADHD adult who freezes on ordinary tasks and carries years of "why can't I just do this" shame.
- The burned-out professional / student in Byung-Chul Han's achievement society, auto-exploiting until they stall.
- Philippines-first: young adults (roughly 18-40), phone-native, heavy social users, price-sensitive, community-oriented. Tagalog/Taglish register lands harder than clinical English.

Their felt state, in their words: "stuck," "frozen," "I know what to do and I still can't," "there's too much," "I'll do it later" (that never comes), "what's the point."

## 3. What the app actually does today (do not invent features)

- **Task Shrinker.** Take one hard task, get 3 to 10 tiny physical steps. Physical verbs only ("put one plate in the sink"), never "plan it out" or "get organized." Deep Shrink (premium) breaks bigger tasks into gentler steps.
- **The one-thing home screen (Next).** Shows a single next action, never a growing list, never a badge.
- **The Room (Sit-With-Me).** A text-based body-double. A quiet otter sits with you while you work, checks in when you need it, knows when to say nothing. No cameras, mic only when you press it.
- **Braindump.** Dump a paragraph, get a short list back. No priorities, no colors.
- **Soft streak.** Seven quiet ripples for days you showed up. No fire, no shame for the days you did not.
- **Energy pill.** Low / medium / good. On low days Otterly picks smaller steps on purpose.
- **Voice capture** turns spoken tasks into text.
- Free forever. Deep Shrink is a one-time purchase or a small monthly. Vouchers for anyone who cannot pay.

## 5. The science, operationalized — the framework mapped to Otterly

The uploaded PDF is the full framework. Here is what each mechanism means for messaging and onboarding. Every post and every onboarding decision should trace to one of these.

**Task paralysis is a regulatory failure, not a character flaw.** Chronic stress raises cortisol, shrinks the prefrontal cortex (the brain's "project manager"), and hypertrophies the amygdala (the threat detector). Acute stress does it faster: "chemistry over circuitry" chemically takes the prefrontal cortex offline.
- Messaging: this is the permission-to-not-be-broken angle. "You are not lazy. Your planning brain is literally offline right now. Otterly does the planning for you." This reframe is Otterly's most powerful and most defensible hook.
- Onboarding: name the state without shame in the first screen. Lead with the feeling, not the feature.

**High arousal must be downregulated BEFORE structure works.** The framework is explicit: "planning or cognitive interventions are often ineffective when an individual is highly stressed." A panicked, frozen user cannot use a task list yet.
- Onboarding gap and opportunity: for a user who arrives frozen/panicked, a task list is the wrong first move. A brief physiological downregulation (paced breathing, one sighing exhale, "let's just breathe for ten seconds") should come BEFORE the Shrinker. This is a real feature/onboarding idea, not just copy.
- Messaging: "Before the to-do list, a breath."

**Cognitive Load Theory.** Working memory holds only 3 to 4 chunks. Overload (intrinsic + extraneous + germane) causes freezing. Extraneous load (clutter, choices, notifications) is the avoidable kind.
- Product proof: the one-thing home screen and the tiny-steps Shrinker literally reduce element interactivity below the freeze threshold. Say this plainly: "One thing on the screen. Never a list."

**Choice overload and decision fatigue.** Iyengar's jam study: 24 options → 3% bought, 6 options → 30% bought. More choice paralyzes and demotivates. The binary-choice rule and satisficing ("good enough") restore action.
- Product proof and hook: Otterly shows ONE thing, and the Shrinker gives a short path, not a menu. "The list is the problem. So we hid it."
- Onboarding: keep onboarding itself near-choiceless. Every extra choice in onboarding is extraneous load on the exact brain that came here because it cannot choose.

**Procrastination is emotion regulation, not time management.** It is short-term mood repair winning over long-term action, reinforced into a shame-inertia loop. The present self offloads onto the future self.
- Messaging: never "stop procrastinating." Instead, "the task feels like a threat, so your brain runs. Otterly makes the first step too small to be a threat."
- Product proof: the soft streak and no-guilt design interrupt the shame loop instead of feeding it.

**Self-Determination Theory.** Motivation runs intrinsic → amotivation ("why bother, my actions make no difference" = full paralysis). Three needs: autonomy, competence, relatedness.
- This is the spine of "Start, Not Alone." Autonomy: you choose, Otterly never nags. Competence: tiny steps manufacture small wins. Relatedness: the Room means you are not doing it alone.
- Onboarding: satisfy all three early. Give a choice (autonomy), deliver one completed micro-step (competence), introduce the otter presence (relatedness).

**Relatedness and loneliness.** APA Stress in America 2025: high loneliness → 70% report difficulty planning, 75% difficulty deciding, 71% neglecting self-care. Loneliness is a functional impairment, not just a mood.
- The Room / body-doubling is the direct answer. Market it as co-regulation, not a chat feature. "A quiet otter to sit with you while you work."

**Byung-Chul Han's achievement society.** Modern people are "entrepreneurs of themselves," auto-exploiting until burnout. Hyperattention destroys the stillness needed to think.
- Positioning: Otterly is the anti-hustle app. It does not gamify your recovery or push notifications at you all day. Say what it will NOT do as loudly as what it does.

**Cognitive appraisal (Lazarus and Folkman) and Yerkes-Dodson.** Stress comes from appraising a task as exceeding your resources; excessive arousal causes "frazzle" and freeze.
- Reappraisal is a lever: shrink the task until it is appraised as manageable, not threatening. That is exactly what the Shrinker does to the appraisal, not just the task.

## 6. The four intervention tiers (use these to structure onboarding and feature messaging)

The framework's intervention model, in the order they must be applied for a stressed user:
1. **Physiological / neuroendocrine regulation** — paced breathing, movement, rest-as-requirement. Comes FIRST for a frozen user.
2. **Attentional and task structuralization** — micro-stepping, next-action-only, externalizing working memory, time-containers. This is the Shrinker and the one-thing home.
3. **Choice and decision simplification** — binary choice, satisficing, hard decision deadlines. This is the hidden list and the short step-path.
4. **Psychological re-appraisal and self-compassion** — self-compassion buffers shame, reframing lowers a task's threat value, "I am excited" reappraises arousal. This is the no-shame tone and the soft streak.

Onboarding should walk a new user down this ladder in this order, especially placing Tier 1 (a breath) before Tier 2 (the first shrink) for anyone who signals they are overwhelmed right now.

## 7. Do-not list

- Do not imply laziness, willpower, or "try harder."
- Do not manufacture guilt, urgency, or FOMO.
- Do not gamify recovery (no streaks-that-punish, no fire emojis, no "don't break the chain").
- Do not promise to cure ADHD or make medical claims. Otterly is a calm task-starter, not a treatment.
- Do not bury the user in choices or steps in onboarding.
- Do not use generic wellness-app aesthetics or clichés.
